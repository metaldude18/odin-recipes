Authentic Korean Bulgogi

What the heck is Bulgogi?

Bulgogi is a Korean dish that literally means fire meat. It can be grilled or roasted and typically is made of thinly sliced and marinated beef or pork. Often bulgogi is stir-fried in a pan at home if outdoor grilling is not possible. Rice or noodles and vegetables are also usually served with it.

Ingredients

  • 1½ pounds beef top sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons white cooking wine
  • 1 cup pear juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Asian (toasted) sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onion
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • ½ pound fresh mushrooms, halved
  • ½ onion, chopped

Steps

  1. Place the beef in a bowl, and pour in the cooking wine, pear juice, and black pepper. Stir to combine, and allow to marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. Stir in the soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, green onion, and sesame seeds, and marinate in refrigerator at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Preheat an outdoor grill or iron skillet on a stovetop for medium-high heat.
  4. Remove the beef from the marinade, and discard the marinade.
  5. Place a sheet of aluminum foil on the heated grill, and lay the beef slices separately on the foil. Place the mushrooms and onion on another part of the foil.
  6. Cook the beef slices until they are evenly brown, 3 to 5 minutes per side. Serve with cooked mushrooms and onion.
